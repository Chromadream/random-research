1. Game Overview & Win Condition

Harvest Moon: Back to Nature (PS1, 1999/2000) is widely considered one of the finest entries in the farming-sim series. You inherit your late grandfather's derelict farm in Mineral Village and have three years to restore it to prosperity, win the trust of the villagers, and (optionally) marry and start a family.

Win Condition: At the end of Year 3, Mayor Thomas evaluates your progress. If you've done well enough (high farm completion percentage, good relationships), you're welcomed as a permanent resident and can continue playing indefinitely. The game never truly ends — it continues past Year 3.

Farm Completion Factors

  • +1% for each animal owned (excluding dog, horse, and fish)
  • +1% for each heart on your dog
  • +1% for each heart on your horse
  • +1% for each heart on your wife
  • +1% for each heart on your child
  • Each building expansion adds percentage
  • Shipping large quantities of crops and winning festivals helps

Core Gameplay Loop

Each day (6:00 AM – 5:50 AM next day), you manage a cycle of: water crops → tend animals → harvest → forage/mine/fish → socialize → sleep. Time pauses inside buildings and the mine, but flows freely outside. The year has four 30-day seasons with season-specific crops, foraging items, and festivals.

3. Year 1 Walkthrough — The Critical First Year

Your Year 1 Goal: Build the Chicken Coop upgrade and the 1st House Extension (Kitchen) before Winter. Accumulate at least 5 chickens, 1–2 cows, and have your chosen bachelorette at Green heart or higher.

🌱 Spring (Year 1)

  • Day 1: Festival day. Clear your field (axe + hammer). Forage and sell everything. Plant 4 bags of Turnips with your starting 500G.
  • Day 2–3: Visit Yodel Ranch to get your free pony. Visit the beach between 7–10 AM to get the free Fishing Rod from Greg. Continue clearing field.
  • Day 4–7: Harvest Turnips, reinvest in Potatoes and Cucumbers. Begin chopping stumps (upgrade Axe to Copper ASAP).
  • Day 8: Goddess Festival — you'll likely go alone first year. Keep working.
  • Day 9–13: Buy as many Cucumber seeds as you can afford. They'll regrow all season.
  • Day 14: Spring Thanksgiving. Without a kitchen, skip or give flowers.
  • Day 15–17: Mayor asks for 3 turnips (or other veggies) by Day 21. Deliver them after 4 PM to his house.
  • Day 18: Horse Race — bet if you like, but you can't race your young pony.
  • Day 19–21: Harvest Potatoes. Chop wood daily. You should be approaching 5,000G and ~420 lumber.
  • Day 22: Cooking Festival — bring a spa-boiled egg to participate.
  • Day 23–30: Upgrade Chicken Coop with Gotz (5,000G + 420 lumber). Buy a chicken from the Poultry Farm (1,500G). Keep foraging, chopping, and mining.

☀️ Summer (Year 1)

  • Day 1: Swimming Festival. Strategy: alternate X (swim) and Triangle (breathe) rapidly. Win for a Power Berry!
  • Day 2: Buy Pineapple seeds from Won (at the Inn, 1–3 PM). Buy as many as possible — they're the best summer crop.
  • Buy a Cow from Yodel Ranch (6,000G) + Milker (2,000G) + Brush (800G). Plant grass for feed.
  • Day 7: Chicken Sumo Festival — bring your happiest hen.
  • Day 12: Tomato Festival — join a team; Ann's and Popuri's teams are strongest.
  • Day 20: Cow Festival — enter your cow if old enough.
  • Day 24: Fireworks — watch with your favorite girl at the beach (6 PM).
  • End of Summer: Upgrade your House (Kitchen) — 4,700G + 370 lumber. This is critical for cooking, gift storage, and marriage progression.

🍂 Fall (Year 1)

  • Buy Sweet Potatoes — lots of them. They're the most profitable crop in the game.
  • Day 3: Music Festival at the Church (6 PM).
  • Day 9: Harvest Festival — bring a Green Pepper for the stew.
  • Day 13: Moon-Viewing — go to Mother's Hill peak at 6 PM.
  • Day 14–20: Duke asks for help harvesting grapes. Bring Cliff to help — this is required for Cliff to stay in town!
  • Day 21: Sheep Festival.
  • Buy a Sheep (4,000G) + Clippers (1,800G). Enter the Sheep Festival — sheep warm up faster than cows.
  • Upgrade Barn if possible. Harvest Sweet Potatoes relentlessly.

❄️ Winter (Year 1)

  • No crops grow outdoors. This is your season for: mining, fishing, chopping lumber, upgrading tools, building friendships, and cooking recipes.
  • Day 10: Dog Race. Train your dog with the Dog Ball (buy from Won in Fall).
  • Day 14: Winter Thanksgiving — girls who like you bring chocolate. Stay at your farm all day!
  • Day 24: Star Night Festival — ask a girl on the 23rd to have dinner with her family.
  • Day 30: New Year's Eve — go to Mother's Hill peak at midnight.
  • Mining: Both the Waterfall Mine (year-round) and Winter Mine are accessible. The Winter Mine has rare ores: Adamantite and Orichalcum.
  • Tool Upgrades: Upgrade Watering Can to Mystrile during winter rain/snow days.
End of Year 1 Target: Kitchen built, 5–8 chickens, 2+ cows, 1+ sheep, all tools at Silver+, bachelorette at Green-Yellow heart, 30+ fish in pond, at least 10 recipes discovered.

11. Secrets & Advanced Tips

🔵 The Kappa Berry (Special Power Berry)

Throw 3 cucumbers into the lake on Mother's Hill (stand in front of the two trees on the left side). Kappa appears. Throw 2 more cucumbers and he'll give you a Special Berry that halves all energy consumption — effectively doubling your work capacity! This is the single most powerful item in the game.

Important: Save before attempting. If you leave after Kappa appears, the cucumber count resets.

🐝 Rare Bees & Honey

Plant at least one 3×3 square of flowers in any season. Bees will build a hive in your apple tree. Collect honey daily (1 bottle). Give one bottle to Louis (lives with Gotz). He'll declare them rare bees, and honey price increases from 50G → 60G. Honey is available year-round once the hive is established.

🌊 Harvest Goddess Offerings

Throw crops/animal products into the oval-shaped pond at the base of the waterfall (near the hot spring). Rewards:

  • 5 offerings: Power Berry
  • 10 offerings: She asks if you have a sweetheart. Answer "Yes" — triggers a hot spring scene with the girl who likes you most!
  • 20 offerings: She gives you a log; Gotz pays you 1,000G for it.

Only works on sunny, non-festival days.

🍳 Spa-Boiled Eggs

Throw regular eggs into the hot spring's rock-crushed corner (where bubbles form). They become Spa-Boiled Eggs, selling for 80G (vs. 50G for regular eggs). Great early-game profit boost!

📺 TV Shopping & Cooking Utensils

Watch the shopping channel on Saturdays. After the show, immediately go to the Inn and use the phone to order (10G call fee). Items arrive in 3 days via Zack. You need these for advanced recipes: Knife, Frying Pan, Pot, Mixer, Whisk, Rolling Pin, Oven, Spice Rack.

🌪️ Weather Manipulation

Save your game, check the next day's weather. If it's a storm/blizzard, reload. The forecast will likely change! Use this to avoid typhoons destroying your crops or to schedule tool upgrades on rainy days.

🐟 Infinite Fishing Trick

Throw fish into your horse's saddlebag (unlimited capacity). Stand near the fish pond, pull fish with X, throw them into the horse's pack. Great for stocking fish to sell.

🌙 Stay Up All Night

Work until 5:50 AM, then jump into the hot spring. At 6:00 AM, the screen fades and you wake up in your house — no stamina penalty! Drink a Turbojolt before 6 AM to be safe.

13. Stamina Management

Stamina vs. Fatigue

  • Stamina depletes as you use tools. Once empty, Fatigue starts building.
  • If Fatigue gets too high, you can collapse — wasting the rest of the day and starting the next day at noon!
  • Working in rain/snow causes fatigue to build even before stamina runs out.

Harvest Sprites System

The 7 sprites live behind the Church. Befriend them with Flour (50G from Supermarket). They can be hired for 1–7 days to:

15. Multi-Run Mastery Plan

Run 1: The Learning Run (Year 1–3)

  • Focus on money accumulation: Cucumbers → Pineapples → Sweet Potatoes
  • Build all expansions in order
  • Marry your favorite bachelorette by Year 2
  • Collect at least 5 Power Berries
  • Save 3 Adamantite in Winter for Maker Machines
  • Win at least the Chicken and Sheep festivals
  • Keep Cliff in town (tell him about the grape harvest job)

Run 2: The Completionist Run

  • Marry a different bachelorette
  • Collect all 10 Power Berries
  • Catch all 6 Legendary Fish
  • Unlock all secret crops (Strawberries, Pumpkins, Spinach, Moon Drop Flowers)
  • Win every festival at least once
  • Build the Hothouse
  • Upgrade all tools to Mystrile
  • Discover all 100+ recipes

Run 3: The Speed/Optimization Run

  • Get kitchen before Cooking Festival Year 1 (very tight — requires optimal play)
  • Have 10 chickens and 5 cows by end of Year 1
  • Get married in Year 1 (requires intense gift-giving)
  • Full barn (20 animals) by end of Year 2

Run 4 & 5: Romance All Bachelorettes

  • Each run, marry a different girl to see all heart events, all wedding scenes, and all rival marriages
  • Experience different baby/child scenarios
