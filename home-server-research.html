Why the i3-12100?

  • Intel UHD Graphics 730 with QuickSync — hardware transcodes 6+ simultaneous 4K → 1080p streams without breaking a sweat.
  • 60W TDP (89W max turbo) — the entire system with 4+ spinning drives idles around 28–45W.
  • 4 cores / 8 threads — enough for Plex, Pi-hole, Home Assistant, Nextcloud, Vaultwarden, and more simultaneously.
  • DDR4 support — keeps RAM costs down. 32 GB is ~AU$90.
  • PCIe Gen5 support — future expansion for 10GbE networking or HBA cards.
⚠️ Important: Do not buy the i3-12100F variant — the "F" means no integrated GPU, which means no QuickSync hardware transcoding. Make sure you get the standard i3-12100.

Power Optimisation Tips

  • Enable C-states in BIOS — deeper sleep states can cut idle power 20–40%.
  • Use an efficient PSU — 80+ Gold rated, and size it for your actual load (not over-provisioned). A 450–550W unit running at 20% load is more efficient than a 750W unit at 10%.
  • Spin down idle HDDs — Unraid's spin-down feature saves ~5–9W per drive overnight.
  • NVMe over SATA SSDs — NVMe drives use 1–2W less at idle per drive.
  • Intel over AMD for idle efficiency — Intel CPUs (especially 12th-gen+) idle significantly lower than comparable AMD chips. A Ryzen 5600X system struggles to get below 70W idle; an i3-12100 system can idle at 28–35W with drives.

💾 Storage Strategy

Recommended Drive Types for Home Server

  • OS / Docker / VMs: 500 GB – 1 TB NVMe SSD (Samsung 970/980 EVO, WD Black SN770). Fast, reliable, separate from media storage.
  • Media Storage: NAS-grade CMR HDDs — WD Red Plus or Seagate IronWolf. Avoid SMR drives (plain WD Red, some Seagate Barracuda) for RAID/ZFS.
  • Parity / Redundancy: Use Unraid with 1–2 parity drives, or TrueNAS with ZFS RAID-Z1/Z2 for data integrity.
⚠️ RAID is NOT a backup. Follow the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies of your data, on 2 different media types, with 1 copy off-site. Back up your server to an external drive or cloud (Backblaze B2, Hetzner Storage Box).
