1. How the Marriage System Works

Marriage in Harvest Moon: Back to Nature is not optional if you want to continue past Year 3. At the end of the third year, Mayor Thomas evaluates your progress. If you haven't married, your rivals will marry the bachelorettes, and your game will end. Marrying a bachelorette is a core part of the win condition.

💡 The Childhood Promise: The opening cutscene shows you as a young boy playing on Mother's Hill with a village girl. You made a promise to return one day. That girl is always the one you end up marrying — the game retroactively identifies her at the end. It's a lovely narrative touch that ties the whole romance arc together.

There are five bachelorettes in Mineral Village, each with:

  • A distinct personality, daily schedule, and family
  • Unique gift preferences (Loved → Liked → Neutral → Disliked → Hated)
  • Three mandatory heart events (Black, Purple, Blue heart level)
  • A rival bachelor who will marry her if you don't act fast enough
  • A primary birthday and an alternate birthday
⚠️ Critical: You must be married before the start of Year 3. If you aren't engaged by the end of Year 2, your chosen bachelorette will marry her rival and you'll be left single — ending your game!

2. Heart Levels & Affection Points (AP)

Each bachelorette tracks your relationship using a hidden Affection Points (AP) counter that ranges from 0 to 65,535. The heart color you see when talking to her represents the range she's currently in.

Heart Color Ranges

🖤 Black Complete Stranger 0 – 4,999 Black Heart Event
💜 Purple Acquaintance / Friend 5,000 – 19,999 Purple Heart Event
💙 Blue Close Friend 20,000 – 29,999 Blue Heart Event
💚 Green She Starts Noticing You 30,000 – 39,999 Changed dialogue; she's more affectionate
💛 Yellow Flirting / Interested 40,000 – 49,999 Winter Thanksgiving: Cake instead of chocolate
🧡 Orange "Dating" 50,000 – 59,999 Blue Feather appears in Supermarket!

📊 How to Check: Simply talk to the bachelorette. The heart icon at the end of her dialogue line shows her current heart color. There's no numeric display in-game — you have to go by the color.

Ways to Raise Affection

  1. Giving gifts — the primary method. Each day, the first gift gives the most AP; subsequent gifts give less.
  2. Talking to her daily — gives a small AP boost each day.
  3. Choosing correct dialogue options during heart events — can boost AP by +2,000 or more.
  4. Attending romantic festivals with her — significant AP boosts for events like the Fireworks Display, Star Night, and Moon-Viewing.
  5. Giving Perfume (available from TV Shopping) — gives a large one-time boost of ~+1,000 AP to all girls!

3. The "Alt Birthday" — Fully Explained 🔍

This is perhaps the single most confusing mechanic in the game, and the original strategy guide didn't explain it at all. Here is the complete explanation:

What "Alt Birthday" Means

Every bachelorette (and many villagers) has two possible birthday dates:

  • Primary Birthday: Her default birthday, used in most playthroughs.
  • Alternate (Alt) Birthday: A backup date used only when the player's birthday falls on the same day as the bachelorette's primary birthday.
🔑 The Rule: When you start the game, Mayor Thomas asks for your birthday. If the birthday you enter happens to land on the same day as a bachelorette's primary birthday, the game shifts her birthday to her alternate date — ensuring you don't have to share a birthday and miss out on the massive birthday gift bonus.

Example Scenario

Scenario: You set your player birthday to Summer 3. Normally, Popuri's birthday is Summer 3. But since your birthday now conflicts, Popuri's birthday shifts to Summer 10 instead. Everyone else's birthdays stay the same.

How to Know Which One Is Active

The game doesn't explicitly tell you which birthday is active. The simplest way to be sure: check the calendar on the wall of your house. On a bachelorette's birthday, the calendar will show her name. If you're unsure, mark both dates and check on each day. If you see villagers gathering or the girl has special dialogue, you've found her birthday. Or, you can simply note your own birthday — if it doesn't conflict with any girl's primary birthday, all primary dates are in effect.

💡 Pro Tip: When setting your birthday at the start of the game, consider choosing a date that doesn't conflict with any bachelorette you're interested in. That way her primary birthday — which is usually more convenient seasonally — remains active. Alternatively, you can deliberately set your birthday to match a girl you don't plan to pursue, keeping the others on their primary dates.

Why This Matters Strategically

Birthday gifts are the single most powerful affection boost in the game:

  • A Loved gift on a birthday gives +2,500 AP (vs. +500 on a normal day) — that's 5× the normal amount!
  • A Wrapped Loved gift on a birthday can give even more.
  • Missing a birthday means waiting an entire in-game year for the next opportunity.
  • Knowing both dates also helps you plan gift stockpiling ahead of time.

⚠️ Warning: Giving a Hated gift on a birthday costs 4,000 AP — that can undo weeks of gift-giving in one mistake! Always double-check what you're handing over on birthdays.

Gift Wrapping

Wrapping Paper can be purchased at the Supermarket for 100G. To wrap a gift, hold the wrapping paper and press the button to use it, then select the item. Wrapped gifts provide an extra affection boost:

  • On birthdays, wrapping adds approximately +2 extra points to the gift's base value (e.g., a Loved gift goes from +45 to +47 in the older scoring model).
  • On normal days, wrapping still provides a modest boost — worth doing for Loved gifts especially.
  • Always wrap birthday gifts! The small cost of 100G is nothing compared to the affection gain.

Multiple Gifts Per Day

Unlike many other Harvest Moon games, you CAN give multiple gifts per day to the same person in Back to Nature. However:

  • The first gift each day gives the most AP.
  • Every subsequent gift gives reduced AP (see table above).
  • Neutral gifts give 0 AP after the first one — don't bother with multiple neutrals.
  • This means you can "rush" affection with 2–3 Loved gifts per day, but it's resource-intensive.

5. Marriage Requirements — Step-by-Step

Step 1: Raise Affection to Orange Heart (50,000+ AP)

Give Loved/Liked gifts daily. Trigger all three heart events. Talk to her every day.

Step 2: The Blue Feather Appears

Once any bachelorette reaches Orange Heart, the Blue Feather appears at the Supermarket for 1,000G. Won also sells it for a ridiculous 30,000G — don't buy from him.

Step 3: Build the 2nd House Extension

Talk to Gotz. You need the 2nd House Extension (10,000G + 750 Lumber, 3 days) which adds a separate bedroom. Then purchase the Double Bed (available after the extension). You cannot marry without the double bed!

Step 4: Reach Red Heart (60,000+ AP)

Continue gifting until her heart turns red. Only at Red Heart will she accept the Blue Feather.

Step 5: Propose!

Equip the Blue Feather (like a tool with Triangle) and present it to her. If she accepts, the feather disappears from your inventory.

Step 6: The Wedding

Exactly 7 days later, the wedding takes place at the Church. A heart icon appears on your calendar marking the date. If a festival conflicts, the wedding is delayed. After the ceremony, she moves into your farmhouse!

⚠️ Build Order Matters: Gotz only offers expansions in a fixed sequence: Chicken Coop Upgrade → 1st House Extension → Barn Upgrade → 2nd House Extension. You cannot skip ahead to the 2nd House Extension. Plan your lumber and cash accordingly!

11. ⚔️ Rival Marriages — The Full Story

Each bachelorette has a rival bachelor who will marry her if you don't propose before Year 3. Understanding how rivals work is crucial for both strategic and narrative reasons.

Rival Overview

BacheloretteRivalRival's LocationThreat LevelNotes
🌸 Popuri Kai Seaside Lodge (Summer only) Low Kai is only in town during Summer. If Popuri marries Kai, both leave Mineral Village permanently and only visit in Summer.
💪 Ann Cliff Inn / Church Medium Cliff will leave the village permanently in Year 2 if you don't tell him about Duke's grape harvest job in Fall. If he stays, he works at the Winery and pursues Ann.
🏥 Elli The Doctor Clinic Medium The Doctor lives and works with Elli daily. Their proximity makes their relationship develop naturally over time.
🍷 Karen Rick Poultry Farm / Inn High Rick is Karen's childhood friend. He's present year-round and shares her evening hangout at the Inn. He argues with her about Kai, but that's just their dynamic.
📚 Mary Gray Blacksmith / Library Medium Gray visits the library regularly to read. He's gruff but softens around Mary. If you befriend Gray, it speeds up his relationship with Mary!
⚠️ Critical Warning — Cliff: In Fall of Year 1, Duke (the winery owner) asks you to help harvest grapes. You MUST talk to Cliff at the Inn and tell him about the job. If you don't, Cliff will permanently leave Mineral Village at the start of Year 2. This not only removes Ann's rival, but also loses a character from the game entirely. The event triggers around Fall 14–20.

Befriending Rivals — The Hidden Danger

Becoming good friends with a rival bachelor accelerates their relationship with the corresponding bachelorette. This means:

  • If you want to marry a girl, do NOT befriend her rival until after your wedding.
  • Giving gifts to the rival, talking to them regularly, and triggering their events all speed up the rival romance.
  • After you're married, feel free to befriend everyone — the rival can then marry the girl (if still single) and that's actually a good outcome for a completionist run.

What Happens If You Don't Marry by Year 3

  • If you haven't proposed by the start of Year 3, each bachelorette will marry her rival.
  • The game ends at the Year 3 evaluation if you're unmarried.
  • If Popuri marries Kai, they leave town. In the girl version (BTN for Girl), you can actually marry Kai yourself!
  • If Karen marries Rick, a special cutscene occurs where they invite you and your wife to their wedding.

Rival Heart Events (For Reference)

These are the rival cutscenes you might witness. They don't directly affect your game unless you're friends with the rival, but watching them helps you gauge how close the rival relationship is:

  • Mary & Gray: Meet at Rose Square, Gray borrows books, Gray returns a book and thanks her
  • Elli & The Doctor: Newborn baby discussion, Elli's cough (Doctor shows concern)
  • Ann & Cliff: Ann flirts with Cliff at the Inn, Ann brings a meal to Cliff at church
  • Karen & Rick: Childhood woods-exploring memory, argument about Kai
  • Popuri & Kai: Summer beach interactions at the Seaside Lodge

13. 🤰 Pregnancy & Children

How Pregnancy Works

After marriage, continue giving your wife gifts daily. Eventually (typically 1–2 seasons after the wedding), she'll start dropping hints:

  • "I feel sick..."
  • "I'm hungry but I don't feel like doing anything..."

Visit the Clinic with her and Elli will confirm the pregnancy. The baby arrives 2 seasons later.

Baby Details

  • The baby is always a boy (no baby girls in BTN).
  • You and your wife name him together.
  • For the first season, he stays in your wife's arms.
  • After about 60 days, the baby grows into a toddler and can crawl around.
  • Give him Milk or Hot Milk to raise his affection.
  • Each heart your child has = +1% farm completion.

15. ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I gave the Blue Feather but she didn't accept. What's wrong?
A: Three possibilities: (1) Her heart isn't truly red — some TVs make orange look like red. Keep gifting. (2) You don't have the 2nd House Extension with double bed. (3) You haven't seen all three heart events.
Q: Can I marry multiple girls?
A: No. You can only marry one. After marriage, other girls' hearts become inaccessible. You can flirt with all five before proposing, but you must pick one. For multiple marriages, you need multiple save files or playthroughs.
Q: What if I don't marry anyone?
A: At the end of Year 3, Mayor Thomas evaluates your farm. Without marriage, your completion percentage is lower (no wife hearts, no child hearts). The game ends and you're not invited to stay. The bachelorettes all marry their rivals.
Q: My game froze after my wife asked what to call me. Help!
A: This is a known bug in the PAL (European) version of the game. After marriage, when your wife asks you to pick a nickname, the game may freeze. Contact the publisher for a replacement disc, or use a different region's version.
Q: Do I need to see rival heart events?
A: No. Rival events are optional and only affect the game if you've befriended the rival, which speeds up their romance. You can safely ignore them — or befriend the rival after your own wedding.
Q: What does the Harvest Goddess hot spring scene do?
A: Offer 10 items to the oval pond at the waterfall base on sunny, non-festival days. After the 10th offering, the Goddess asks if you have a sweetheart. Say "Yes" and the girl who likes you most joins you in the hot spring — a hilarious scene that greatly boosts her affection. This can guarantee a Goddess Festival date in Year 1!
Q: How long does it take to reach Red Heart?
A: With daily Loved gifts (500 AP each), reaching Red Heart (60,000 AP from 0) takes about 120 days — roughly one full in-game year. With birthday bonuses (2,500 AP), correct heart event answers (+2,000 each), and festival participation, you can reach Red Heart by mid-to-late Year 2 even with casual play.
Q: Does my wife help on the farm after marriage?
A: Unfortunately, no. In Back to Nature, your wife stays at home and doesn't help with farm work. This was improved in later Harvest Moon games. She does talk to you, give you occasional dialogue about the baby, and contributes to your farm completion percentage.
