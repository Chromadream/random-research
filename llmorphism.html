LLMorphism

When humans come to see themselves as language models
Valerio Capraro · University of Milano-Bicocca · arXiv:2605.05419 · May 2026
Computers & Society (cs.CY)

What is LLMorphism?

LLMorphism is the biased belief that human cognition works like a large language model. It is a specific form of mechanomorphism — the attribution of machine characteristics to humans — narrowed to the particular case of LLMs.

"The issue is not only whether we are attributing too much mind to machines, but also whether we are beginning to attribute too little mind to humans."

Conversational LLMs make this bias psychologically available through a reverse inference: we already anthropomorphize LLMs (they speak like humans → they must have minds). LLMorphism flips this — LLMs speak like humans → perhaps humans think like LLMs.

Two Mechanisms of Spread

Analogical Transfer

Observable similarity between human and LLM language triggers structural alignment. What starts as comparing linguistic output becomes a broader reinterpretation of cognition as LLM-like generation, prediction, and pattern completion.

Metaphorical Availability

Technical vocabulary migrates into ordinary self-understanding. Terms like "prediction," "prompting," "hallucination," "training data," and "next-token prediction" become culturally salient descriptors for human thought, creativity, and memory.

These mechanisms are mutually reinforcing.

What It Is Not

Anthropomorphism

Treating machines as human-like (LLM as person). LLMorphism is the reverse: treating humans as machine-like.

Computationalism

The thesis that cognition is symbol-manipulation. LLMorphism is about fluency and language generation, not rule-based computation.

Dehumanization

Denial of humanness with hostility/moral exclusion. LLMorphism need not involve contempt and can be directed at oneself.

Predictive Processing

Bayesian theories of prediction-error minimization. These are often deeply embodied — LLMorphism is not.

Five Societal Impact Pathways

These are hypotheses, not established facts — the paper introduces a new construct.
  • Work Replaceability. If humans are seen as LLM-like output generators, automation appears not just efficient but conceptually justified. Workers become statistically replaceable text-producing units.
  • Education Fluency Confusion. If LLMs approximate expert outputs, institutions may mistake expertise for sophisticated text production. Learning becomes equated with producing fluent answers, even though linguistic form ≠ grounded understanding.
  • Responsibility Agency Thinning. If human action is redescribed as "generated output from prior inputs," questions about reasons, commitments, and accountability are displaced by a flatter vocabulary of input/output/generation. Erodes responsibility practices.
  • Healthcare Disembodiment. Verbal output may be overweighted relative to embodied and affective cues. Patients become "producers of clinically relevant text" rather than embodied persons. Risk is acute in mental health, where nonverbal signs are critical.
  • Epistemic Fluency as Knowledge. If human reasoning is "just pattern completion," justification and evidence lose normative force. Language model vocabulary blurs the distinction between saying and knowing — fluency substitutes for epistemic evaluation.

Why the Inference Is Biased

Similarity in linguistic output does not entail similarity in cognitive architecture.

Human language is a tool for communication developed by embodied organisms with needs, emotions, memories, social obligations, and vulnerability to consequences. LLM output is generated by systems trained to model statistical regularities in text — there is no perception, action, affect, intention, or accountability behind it.

LLMs have strong formal linguistic competence (grammatical, coherent text) but limited functional linguistic competence (using language in the world, which requires reasoning, world knowledge, situation modeling, social cognition).

Boundary Conditions & Resistance

Several forces resist LLMorphism:

Salient Disanalogies

When people attend to differences between humans and LLMs (embodiment, affect, accountability), analogical transfer weakens.

Alternative Metaphors

Essentialist, religious, or humanistic worldviews provide competing frameworks that reject the LLM metaphor outright.

Professional Experience

Caregiving roles (nursing, therapy, early childhood education) confront practitioners with aspects of human life that resist LLM-style description.

Future Research Directions

The paper proposes four potential dimensions of LLMorphism for future measurement:

  • Language generation: belief that thought/speech is next-token prediction
  • Learning & creativity: belief that humans learn via statistical regularities and creativity is recombination
  • Self-understanding: belief that introspection and explanation are post-hoc narratives
  • Truth-seeking: belief that reasoning aims at plausibility, not truth

Open questions: Does greater LLM exposure increase or decrease LLMorphism? Are there gender, education, or occupation effects? Can experimental manipulation of LLM exposure shift how people describe human cognition?
