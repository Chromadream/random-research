1. How the Marriage System Works (FoMT vs BTN)

Marriage in Harvest Moon: Friends of Mineral Town is a central part of the experience. Unlike Back to Nature, you are not evaluated after 3 years — you can marry at your own pace!

💡 Key FoMT Differences from BTN:
• No time limit — you can marry in Year 1, Year 5, or never. There's no game-over evaluation.
• New heart events — completely rewritten from BTN! Locations, times, and dialogues are different.
• Harvest Goddess is marriageable — an entirely new challenge requiring near-100% completion.
• Gift preferences differ — each girl's loves/likes/hates were adjusted from BTN.
• The childhood girl is never revealed — it's left ambiguous in FoMT.
• Rival marriages take 4 years — much slower than BTN, giving you plenty of time.

There are five bachelorettes plus the Harvest Goddess in Mineral Village, each with:

  • A distinct personality, daily schedule, and family
  • Unique gift preferences (Special → Loved → Liked → Neutral → Disliked → Hated)
  • Four mandatory heart events (Black, Purple, Blue, Yellow)
  • A rival bachelor who will marry her if you wait too long (Year 4+)
  • A primary birthday and an alternate birthday
📊 Extra Relationship Points: Each girl has a way to earn a one-time +200 affection point bonus. For Ann: buy anything from the Inn. For Elli: get examined by the Doctor. For Karen: buy items from the Supermarket. For Mary: go upstairs in the Library. For Popuri: own 8 chickens.

2. Heart Levels & Affection Points (AP)

Each bachelorette tracks your relationship using a hidden Affection Points (AP) counter ranging from 0 to 65,535. The heart color you see when talking to her represents the range she's in.

💚 Green She Starts Noticing You 30,000 – 39,999 Can invite to Fireworks/Full Moon/Star Night festivals

🧡 Orange "Dating" 50,000 – 59,999 Blue Feather appears in Supermarket!
❤️ Red In Love — Ready to Propose 60,000 – 65,535 Can propose with Blue Feather

Ways to Raise Affection

  1. Giving gifts — the primary method. Each day, the first gift gives the most AP; subsequent gifts give less (~1/3 normal).
  2. Talking to her daily — gives a small AP boost each day.
  3. Choosing correct dialogue options during heart events — can boost AP by +3,000 or more.
  4. Attending romantic festivals with her — significant AP boosts for Fireworks Display, Full Moon, and Star Night.
  5. Extra relationship points — each girl has a unique action that gives +200 points (once).

3. The "Alt Birthday" — Fully Explained 🔍

What "Alt Birthday" Means

Every bachelorette has two possible birthday dates:

  • Primary Birthday: Her default birthday, used in most playthroughs.
  • Alternate (Alt) Birthday: A backup date used only when the player's birthday falls on the same day as the bachelorette's primary birthday.
🔑 The Rule: When you start the game and set YOUR birthday, if it happens to land on the same day as a bachelorette's primary birthday, the game shifts her birthday to her alternate date — ensuring you don't share a birthday and miss out on the massive birthday gift bonus.

💡 Pro Tip: When setting your birthday, choose a date that doesn't conflict with the girl you want to pursue. This keeps her primary birthday active — which is usually more convenient seasonally. Or, deliberately set your birthday to match a girl you don't plan to pursue.

Why Birthdays Matter

Birthday gifts are the single most powerful affection boost in the game: a Loved gift on a birthday gives +2,500 AP (vs. +500 on a normal day) — 5× the normal amount! With wrapping paper, the bonus is even higher. Missing a birthday means waiting an entire in-game year for the next opportunity.

  • Always wrap birthday gifts! The small cost of 100G is nothing compared to the affection gain.

5. Marriage Requirements — Step-by-Step

Step 1: Choose Your Bachelorette & Start Gifting

Talk to her daily. Give Loved/Liked gifts. Trigger the Black Heart Event (available from 0 AP). Continue until Purple heart.

Step 2: Trigger All Four Heart Events

Black → Purple → Blue → Yellow. Each unlocks at a specific heart color. You MUST view these in order. Blue event requires Large Rucksack + empty item slot. Yellow requires Yellow heart+.

Step 3: Build Farm Infrastructure

Upgrade house twice (3,000G+200 Lumber, then 10,000G+700 Lumber). Buy the Big Bed from TV Shopping (10,010G). Purchase all kitchen utensils.

Step 4: Buy the Blue Feather

Once any bachelorette reaches Orange Heart, the Blue Feather appears at the Supermarket for 1,000G. Won also sells it for 30,000G — don't buy from him!

Step 5: Reach Red Heart (60,000+ AP)

Continue gifting until her heart turns Red. Only at Red Heart will she accept the Blue Feather.

Step 6: Propose!

Equip the Blue Feather (like a tool with B) and present it to her. Make sure you've seen ALL four heart events and have the Big Bed!

Step 7: The Wedding

Exactly 7 days later, the wedding takes place at the Church. A heart icon marks the date. If a festival conflicts, the wedding is delayed. After the ceremony, she moves into your farmhouse!

⚠️ Common Proposal Rejections: If she refuses: (1) Her heart may not truly be Red (some screens make Orange look like Red). Keep gifting. (2) You haven't seen all four heart events. (3) You don't have the Big Bed yet. (4) You haven't done the second house extension.

11. 🌟 Marrying the Harvest Goddess FoMT EXCLUSIVE (Difficulty: Near Impossible)

⚠️ The Ultimate Challenge: Marrying the Harvest Goddess requires near-100% completion of the entire game. Most players will never achieve this. Only attempt if you're truly dedicated!

Requirements

  1. Catch all species of fish — every fish in the game, including all 6 King Fish
  2. Ship at least 1 of every shippable item — complete the entire shipping list
  3. Collect all 9 Goddess Jewels from the Spring Mine → forms the Goddess Gem
  4. Collect all 9 Kappa Jewels from the Lake Mine → forms the Kappa Gem
  5. Collect all 9 Jewels of Truth → forms the Gem of Truth
  6. Dig up every type of item from both the Spring Mine and Lake Mine (including Mythic Stones!)
  7. Own the Big Bed and have the 2nd house extension
  8. Be in Year 5 or later
  9. Ask Carter for permission during Confessional: "I want to marry the HG"
💡 Goddess Notes: The Harvest Goddess does NOT move into your house after marriage. She stays in her pond. She CAN get pregnant like the other girls. Her birthday is Spring 8 (Alt: Spring 9). She loves Pineapple and Strawberry (500 AP). She likes all crops at 300 AP. She HATES cooked items, mine items, garbage, and Pirate Treasure.

16. ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I gave the Blue Feather but she didn't accept. What's wrong?
A: Three possibilities: (1) Heart isn't truly red — on some screens Orange looks like Red. Keep gifting. (2) Haven't seen all four heart events (Black, Purple, Blue, Yellow). (3) Don't have the Big Bed yet.
Q: Can I marry multiple girls?
A: No. One wife per save file. After marriage, other girls' hearts become inaccessible. For multiple marriages, use multiple save files or playthroughs.
Q: What if I don't marry anyone?
A: Unlike BTN, nothing bad happens! FoMT has no time limit and no evaluation. You can play forever as a bachelor. Eventually, the bachelorettes will marry their rivals around Year 4.
Q: Do I need to see rival heart events?
A: No. Rival events are optional. However, if you want to see rival weddings, you must avoid raising your chosen bachelorette's heart too high, or the rival events won't trigger (the game prioritizes your relationship).
Q: How long does it take to reach Red Heart?
A: With daily Loved gifts (500 AP each), reaching Red (60,000 from 0) takes about 120 days — roughly one in-game year. With birthday bonuses (+2,500), correct heart event answers (+3,000 each), and festival participation, you can reach Red by mid-to-late Year 2 even with casual play.
Q: Does my wife help on the farm after marriage?
A: No. Your wife stays at home in FoMT. She may occasionally cook breakfast for you (restoring stamina), but she doesn't help with farm work. If you marry Elli or Mary, they continue working part-time at the Clinic/Library.
Q: Can I get married in Year 1?
A: Technically yes, but it's very difficult. You need the 2nd house extension, Big Bed, all four heart events, and Red heart — all achievable by late Winter Year 1 with intense optimization. Most players marry in Year 2.
Q: How do I trigger Blue Heart Events?
A: You MUST own the Large Rucksack (8 item slots) AND have at least one empty item slot. The Blue Heart Event gives you a memento item that you keep permanently (Ann's Music Box, Elli's Pressed Flower, Karen's Wine, Mary's Great Book, Popuri's Mud Ball).
Q: What if my birthday conflicts with a girl's birthday?
A: Her birthday shifts to the alternate date (see section 3). Check the calendar on your house wall — on her birthday, her name will appear. If you're unsure, mark both dates and check each day.
